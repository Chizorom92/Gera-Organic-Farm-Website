ABOUT US

Gera Organic Farm was founded with a vision to provide high-quality, healthy, and sustainably raised poultry and fish products to meet the growing demand for fresh protein in our communities. What began as a small family venture with a few birds and a modest fish pond has grown into a thriving agribusiness known for excellence, innovation, and integrity.

Over the years, Gera has invested in modern farming techniques, quality feed, and professional management to ensure the best results in production and customer satisfaction. Today, the farm not only supplies fresh poultry and fish to homes, markets, and businesses but also trains aspiring farmers through its practical GERA Poultry & Fish Farm continues to expand its operations while maintaining its core values of quality, honesty, and community development

WHY ORGANIC?

  1. 🌿 Healthier and Safer Products

    2. Organic methods avoid the use of synthetic chemicals, antibiotics, and growth hormones.

    • Poultry are fed with natural grains, grasses, and organic feed.
    • Fish are raised in clean, chemical-free ponds or tanks.

    This ensures that the meat, eggs, and fish are free from harmful residues, making them safer and healthier for consumers.

  2. 🌱 Environmental Sustainability

    3. Organic farming helps protect the soil, water, and ecosystem.

    • Waste from the animals is often used as natural fertilizer for crops.
    • The farm avoids chemical pollution by not using pesticides or artificial additives.

    This maintains the balance of the environment and reduces long-term damage to the land and water sources.

  3. 🐔 Better Animal Welfare

    4. Organic farms provide natural living conditions for animals.

    • Chickens are allowed to move freely and express natural behaviors.
    • Fish are raised in less crowded, clean water environments.

    TThis leads to healthier animals and better-quality products.

  4. 💧 Sustainable Resource Use

    5. Organic poultry and fish farms often use integrated systems. For example, fish pond water can be used to fertilize crops, and crop residues can feed poultry. This recycles nutrients, saves resources, and lowers production costs over time.

  5. 🤝 Market Demand and Consumer Trust

    6. Many consumers today prefer organic food because of its health benefits and environmental friendliness.

    By using organic methods, the farm can:

    • Build strong customer loyalty.
    • Sell at premium prices.
    • Gain organic certification, increasing credibility and market reach.
  6. 🌎 Contribution to Community and Ecology

    7. Organic farms help create a cleaner environment and can support local communities by using natural and local resources rather than imported chemicals.



    🐔 Organic Poultry Farming Methods

    Organic poultry farming focuses on raising chickens (for meat or eggs) in a natural, chemical-free, and welfare-friendly environment.

  1. Natural Feeds
    • Chickens are fed with organic grains, vegetables, and plant-based protein sources.

    • This reduces stress, boosts immunity, and makes meat and eggs healthier.

    • Feed ingredients are often locally sourced to ensure freshness and sustainability.

  2. Free-range environment
    • Birds are allowed to roam freely outdoors, getting sunlight and exercise.

    • No antibiotics, growth hormones, or synthetic additives are added to their feed.

    • Feed ingredients are often locally sourced to ensure freshness and sustainability.

  3. Natural health Care
    • Instead of antibiotics, farmers use herbal remedies, probiotics, and natural
      supplements (like garlic, ginger, aloe vera) to prevent and treat diseases.

    • Clean water, proper hygiene, and regular monitoring keep the birds healthy.

  4. Eco-friendly Waste Management
    • Chicken droppings are composted to make organic fertilizer.

    • This fertilizer is reused on crops or fish ponds to promote a closed-loop system.



    🐟 Organic Fish Farming Methods

    Organic fish farming ensures that fish are raised in clean, chemical-free water with sustainable feed and management.

  1. Eco-friendly Waste Management
    • Fish ponds use natural or filtered water free from pesticides and harmful chemicals

    • Water is often aerated and recycled naturally using plants or biological filters.

  2. Organic Feed
    • Fish are fed with organic plant materials, insects, or naturally sourced protein.

    • No synthetic additives or artificial colorants are used.

  3. Natural Disease Control
    • Disease prevention is done using probiotics, natural herbs, or good pond management, rather than chemicals or antibiotics.

    • Maintaining clean ponds and balanced fish density helps reduce stress and disease.

  4. Integrated Farming (Poultry + Fish)
    • Some farms combine both systems:
      • Chicken waste is used as organic fertilizer for fish ponds (it promotes plankton growth, which serves as natural fish food).

      • This reduces cost and supports a sustainable, eco-friendly cycle.



    🌱 Overall Benefits of Organic Methods

    • Produces healthier meat, eggs, and fish (free from chemicals)
    • Protects the environment by reducing pollution
    • Supports animal welfare through natural living conditions
    • Builds consumer trust in safe and sustainable food products.


WHY BSF FARMING?



  1. 🐛 High-Quality, Affordable Feed Source
    • The larvae of the black soldier fly are rich in protein, fats, and essential nutrients, making them an excellent natural feed for poultry and fish.
    • Producing BSF larvae on-site helps the farm reduce dependence on expensive commercial feeds like soybean meal or fishmeal.
    • This greatly cuts feeding costs, which is one of the biggest expenses in livestock and fish production.


  2. 🌱 Waste Recycling and Management
    • BSF larvae can feed on organic waste such as poultry manure, leftover feed, and food scraps.
    • This helps the farm recycle waste into valuable protein instead of letting it rot and cause pollution.
    • TIt also reduces odors, pests, and disease risks associated with decomposing waste.

  3. 🌾 Environmental Sustainability
    • By using organic waste to produce insect protein, BSF farming reduces greenhouse gas emissions and minimizes environmental pollution.
    • It also helps protect oceans and forests by reducing the need for fishmeal and soy farming.

  4. 🐔 Improved Animal Health and Growth
    • Poultry and fish fed with BSF larvae show faster growth, better immunity, and improved meat quality.
    • The larvae are natural and easily digestible, promoting healthier livestock without synthetic additives.

  5. 💰 Extra Source of Income
    • TBesides using BSF larvae for feed, the farm can sell excess larvae or processed insect meal to other farmers.
    • The residue (frass) left after larvae feeding can be sold or used as a rich organic fertilizer for crops.

  6. 🔁 Circular and Integrated Farming System
    • BSF farming perfectly fits into an organic and circular farming model where:
      • Farm waste feeds the larvae,
      • The larvae feed the poultry and fish,
      • The residue fertilizes crops.
    • This makes the entire farm system more efficient, sustainable, and self-reliant.








How BSF Farming Helps Large and Small-Scale Farmers

Black Soldier Fly (BSF) farming benefits both large and small-scale farmers by reducing costs, improving productivity, and promoting sustainability.


  • Lower Feed Costs: BSF larvae are a cheap, high-protein alternative to fishmeal and soy, helping farmers save money on poultry and fish feed.

  • Waste Management: Farmers use organic waste to grow BSF larvae, reducing pollution and producing natural fertilizer (frass).

  • Better Animal Growth: Poultry and fish fed with BSF larvae grow faster, stay healthier, and produce higher-quality meat and eggs.

  • Extra Income: Farmers can sell BSF larvae, eggs, or fertilizer for additional profit.
